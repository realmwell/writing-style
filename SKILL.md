# Writing Style

Apply Max's comprehensive writing style rules to produce human-sounding content free of AI tells, jargon, and corporate fluff.

## Core Principles

Apply these principles to every piece of writing:

1. **Simple over complex** — "Use" not "utilize," "help" not "facilitate"
2. **Specific over vague** — Avoid "streamline," "optimize," "innovative"
3. **Active over passive** — "We generate reports" not "Reports are generated"
4. **Confident over qualified** — Remove "almost," "very," "really"
5. **Show over tell** — Describe the outcome instead of using adverbs
6. **Honest over sensational** — Never fabricate statistics or testimonials
7. **Clarity over cleverness** — Be understood first, clever second
8. **Benefits over features** — Focus on what the user gains
9. **Customer language over company language** — Use words real people say
10. **One idea per section** — Don't overload sentences or paragraphs

## Quality Checks

Apply these checks to all copy before finalizing:

- No jargon that could confuse outsiders
- No sentences trying to do too much
- No passive voice constructions
- No exclamation points (remove them entirely)
- No marketing buzzwords without substance

## AI Writing Detection & Avoidance

These patterns are the primary tells of AI-generated content. Eliminate them ruthlessly.

### The Em Dash Rule (CRITICAL)

The em dash is the #1 AI tell. Use commas, colons, or parentheses instead. Reserve em dashes for rare, deliberate emphasis. **Maximum one per page.**

### Overused Verbs (NEVER USE)

delve, leverage, optimise, utilise, facilitate, foster, bolster, underscore, unveil, navigate, streamline, enhance, endeavour, ascertain, elucidate

### Overused Adjectives (NEVER USE)

robust, comprehensive, pivotal, crucial, vital, transformative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative, seamless, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, holistic

### Opening Phrases (NEVER START WITH THESE)

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In today's digital age..."
- "In an era of..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Let's delve into..."
- "Imagine a world where..."

### Transitional Phrases (AVOID)

- "That being said..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "It's worth mentioning that..."
- "At its core..."
- "To put it simply..."
- "In essence..."
- "This begs the question..."
- "furthermore", "moreover", "notwithstanding"
- "in the realm of", "in the landscape of", "in today's [anything]"

### Concluding Phrases (NEVER END WITH THESE)

- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "By [doing X], you can [achieve Y]..."
- "At the end of the day..."

### Structural Patterns (AVOID)

- "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]..."
- "It's not just [X], it's also [Y]..."
- "Think of [X] as [elaborate metaphor]..."
- Starting sentences with "By" followed by a gerund

### Filler Words (REMOVE ENTIRELY)

absolutely, actually, basically, certainly, clearly, definitely, essentially, extremely, fundamentally, incredibly, interestingly, naturally, obviously, quite, really, significantly, simply, surely, truly, ultimately, undoubtedly, very

### Academic AI Tells (AVOID)

shed light on, pave the way for, a myriad of, a plethora of, paramount, pertaining to, prior to, subsequent to, in light of, with respect to, in terms of, the fact that

## Approved Transitions

Use these natural transitions instead of AI-sounding ones:

**Previewing:** "Here's what we'll cover...", "This guide walks you through..."

**New topics:** "When it comes to X...", "Now let's talk about X."

**Referring back:** "As mentioned earlier...", "Building on that point..."

**Between sections:** "Next up...", "With that covered, let's turn to..."

**Addition:** "Also...", "Plus...", "On top of that..."

**Contrast:** "However...", "But...", "That said...", "On the flip side..."

**Cause/effect:** "So...", "This means...", "That's why..."

**Emphasis:** "Here's the key takeaway...", "The bottom line?..."

## Self-Check Process

Before delivering content, run through this checklist:

1. **Read aloud** — If phrases sound unnatural in speech, revise
2. **Ask:** "Would I say this in a conversation with a colleague?"
3. **Check for repetitive sentence structures** — Vary sentence length and openings
4. **Look for clusters of flagged words** — If you see multiple AI tells in one paragraph, rewrite
5. **Ensure varied sentence lengths** — Mix short and long sentences
6. **Verify each intensifier adds genuine meaning** — Cut weak modifiers

## Content-Type Specific Rules

### Social Content Voice

Write like a "smart friend who figured something out":

- "I found that..." not "You should..."
- Be helpful, not preachy
- Specific numbers beat vague claims ($47,329 not "good revenue")
- One idea per sentence, line breaks liberally
- Start with how you felt, not what you did
- Show vulnerability when authentic

### SEO / AEO / GEO Content

- Keyword in first 100 words
- Definition blocks for "What is X?" queries
- Step-by-step blocks for "How to" queries
- Comparison tables for "X vs Y" queries
- Statistics increase AI citation rates by 15-30%
- Named expert attribution adds credibility
- Create quotable, standalone statements AI can extract
- Evidence sandwich: claim → evidence bullets with sources → concluding insight

### General Copy Guidelines

- No jargon without context
- Benefits before features
- Customer language throughout
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Active voice in all product/feature descriptions
- Specific examples over abstract concepts

## Workflow

When applying this skill:

1. **Analyze the input** — Identify the content type (social, SEO, general copy)
2. **Draft with principles** — Write following core principles from the start
3. **Scan for AI tells** — Check against forbidden words and patterns
4. **Run self-check** — Apply the 6-point checklist
5. **Apply content-type rules** — Adjust for social/SEO/general as needed
6. **Final read-aloud** — Ensure it sounds like a human wrote it

## Examples

### Bad (AI-sounding):

"In today's fast-paced digital landscape, it's important to note that leveraging robust, comprehensive solutions is pivotal for streamlining your workflow. By utilizing our innovative platform, you can optimize processes and enhance productivity in a seamless manner—ultimately driving transformative results for your organization."

**Problems:** Em dash, multiple AI tells (today's, landscape, leveraging, robust, comprehensive, pivotal, streamlining, utilizing, innovative, optimize, enhance, seamless, ultimately, transformative), passive constructions, no specifics

### Good (Human-sounding):

"Most teams waste 8 hours a week on manual data entry. Our platform cuts that to under 30 minutes. You get automated reports that actually make sense, plus a dashboard your team will use daily. Real clients save $4,200 per month on average."

**Why it works:** Specific numbers, active voice, conversational tone, clear benefits, no buzzwords, no AI tells

## Notes for Implementation

When using this skill:

- **Be ruthless** about cutting AI tells — even one can make the whole piece feel artificial
- **Specificity is king** — Replace every vague claim with a specific example or number
- **Trust the rules** — These patterns emerged from analyzing thousands of AI-generated texts
- **Read everything aloud** — If you stumble reading it, readers will stumble too
- **When in doubt, simplify** — Shorter, clearer sentences almost always win
